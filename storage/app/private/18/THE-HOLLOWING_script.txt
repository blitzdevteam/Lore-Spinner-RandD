THE HOLLOWING
When the Beast of Bray Road resurfaces during Elkhorn’s cryptid
festival, four Ho-Chunk teens and a Ho-Chunk Nation detective
uncover the decades-old development cover-up that created it—and
must expose the truth before the face-stealing presence claims
another generation.

EXT. RURAL TRAILER - ELKHORN WISCONSIN – NIGHT - 2015
Razor-sharp wind slices through dark pines. A weathered
trailer hunches at the forest's edge, a single window glowing
with fragile candlelight. A lone heartbeat in the wilderness.
INT. TRAILER – NIGHT
Cramped interior breathes with history. Leather-bound books,
carved wooden keepsakes crowd shelves — but so do chipped
mugs. A tribal newsletter buried under a stack of mail spread
out of a small table, as a tea kettle hums quietly in the
tiny kitchen.
A small flat screen TV - muted - playing a college football
game flickers in the corner.
KAI BLACKDEER (7), Ho-Chunk, huddles in a thick blanket,
sketchbook pressed against his knees. His gaze never leaves
his grandfather.
LUTHER BLACKDEER (70s), Ho-Chunk elder, carves a wood stick
with surgical precision. His hands know the shape before the
blade touches — weathered fingers moving with muscle memory.
His eyes drift somewhere between present and memory.
KAI
Grandpa, is it true there’s
something in that hollow?
Luther doesn’t look up.
LUTHER
Some places exist outside time. You
feel them before seeing them. When
your hair lifts and your chest
tightens... that’s not fear. That’s
this place remembering what
happened here — and your body
hearing it.
KAI
Is it the beast?
LUTHER
(chuckles, dry)
That’s what the town calls it. Puts
it on T-shirts. Parades it through
the street.
He gets up from his chair, a hissing tea kettle in the
background. He pours hot water into two small Minnesota
Viking mugs on the stove.

LUTHER(CONT’D)
But names aren’t always protection.
Sometimes... they’re invitations.
Kai leans in as Luther gives him a mug of hot chocolate, then
retreats back to his chair.
KAI
So why do people forget?
LUTHER
Because forgetting’s easy.
(beat)
And when we forget the warnings...
we feed the thing that waits where
the ground never healed.
Kai sips the hot chocolate.
LUTHER(CONT’D)
The stories weren’t told to scare
us. They were so we’d remember what
happened — and stay away from
places that don’t forget.
He continues carving the walking stick.
KAI
When does the beast come out?
Luther stops carving. Looks at him.
LUTHER
When the ground remembers.
(beat)
And it doesn’t forget the ones who
woke it.
KAI
If it remembers us, can we make it
forget?
He taps the sketchbook with his knife handle, a faint smile,
proud.
LUTHER
So you draw it, hm? Better than
letting it sit in the dark with no
name at all.
