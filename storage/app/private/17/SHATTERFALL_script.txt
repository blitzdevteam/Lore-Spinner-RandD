EXT. OUTER DISTRICT – NIGHT
A city in ruin. Smoke weaves through hollow towers. The air
hums alive with distant gunfire, the moan of broken wind.
Ash falls. Slow. Relentless.
Through it walks RAVI "RAZOR" SHAN, 30s, built from scar
tissue and silence. Eyes forward. Movements surgical. A blade
disguised as a man.
He stops. Waits. Shadows move in the near distance. ASH
COVENANT ENFORCERS shake down a family in a crumbling alley.
Screams muffled. Boots striking bone.
Ravi watches. No blink. No twitch. Cold rage trapped under
the skin.
A DOG barks from a rooftop. Ravi locks eyes with it.
The dog goes silent.
He kneels. Finds a broken piece of RED CHALK near a child's
scrawled mural, crude images of soldiers and burning homes.
Ravi pockets the chalk. Stands. Turns to a tenement swallowed
by shadow.
Its door bears a smeared symbol. A triangle bisected by a
single red slash. The mark of an ASH BISHOP.
Ravi studies it, unmoving. A faint echo, ALINA’S LAUGHTER,
drifts through his mind, fractured and fading.
He presses a hand to the door. Enters.
