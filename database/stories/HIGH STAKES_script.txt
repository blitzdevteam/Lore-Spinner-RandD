EXT. WOODED FOREST - DAY
Lush four acre MEADOW with COLORFUL TREES and MOUNTAINS
surrounding miles of WILDERNESS.
Encapsulated by two MOONS hanging brightly in the sky.
One full. The other a half crest.
Everything peaceful and quiet, until-- the ground begins
shaking. Stronger. Harder.
TREES and SOIL crack. Turning over as a thirty foot
STAINED GLASS DOME slowly rises to the surface.
BOOM! One entire side BLOWS COMPLETELY OFF, sending
thousands of SHARDS into the air. Along with TYE
ERICKSON, 26, wearing CAMOUFLAGE CLOTHING and a HIGH TECH
WATCH with BLINKING LIGHT.
Clutching an overloaded CANVAS SACK.
That RIPS OPEN when he painfully lands on his back.
Knocking the wind out of him.
Spilling four, 6 POUND UNCUT DIAMOND ROCKS across the
ground.
TYE
Get up.
Quickly checking the time.
NUMBERS on his WATCH COUNTING BACKWARDS toward ZERO. Only
1 MINUTE 27 SECONDS left.
Forcing Tye to leap to his feet and scoop the DIAMONDS
back into the tattered BAG. Just as a small two-pronged
METALLIC FORK flies past his head--
BOOM! Cutting a TREE in half. Sending him tumbling
backwards into the brush.
Followed by an eerie silence. Save for the RINGING IN HIS
EARS.
Which Tye ignores. Standing. Still holding the treasure
laden SACK. Gathering enough strength to limp off into
the FOREST.
Skillfully maneuvering through FERNS, TWISTING VINES and
TREES finally seeing--
A plain, simple WOOD DOOR. Standing all alone. Ten yards
ahead.

TYE
(relieved)
There you are.
Making a run for it. Out of breath. Reaching for its worn
BRASS HANDLE then BOOM!
He’s HIT square in the back with a METALLIC FORK. CUTTING
TYE IN HALF.
DIAMOND ROCKS spilling across the ground.
As the NUMBERS on his WATCH reach 00:00. The BLINKING
LIGHT stops.
And the WOOD DOOR instantly disappears.
DISSOLVE TO:
INT. NEW YORK STOCK EXCHANGE - DAY
A large CLOCK prominently looms above the TRADING FLOOR,
reading 2:58 PM. With the chaotic ROAR of a frenzied
crowd just below it.
BROKERS and TRADERS racing around like angry bees trying
to close that last minute deal.
One such Trader stands out. More confident, calm, not
sweating like the others. SABASTION “Bash” KRAUSE, 27,

physical shape, handsomely rugged and looks great in a
tailored suit.
Because Bash thrives on this madness. The more the
better.
Scoring one final deal just as the CLOSING BELL RINGS.
Quickly bringing all the chaos to a sudden stop.
Followed by spent and weary Traders staggering off the
floor. Leaving a mess of digital dreams in their wake.
Including frazzled and uptight TODD STEINBERG 25, making
his way through the CROWD over to a clam Bash.
TODD
What a disaster! I barely made it
out alive. Tell me you finally
lost money.
BASH

sometime. No one’s this lucky.

might surprise yourself.
Casually walking away. Todd right behind him. Hoping some
of Bash’s good luck will rub off.
TODD
Any word from John or Tye?
Quickly putting an end to Bash’s energetic face. Replaced
with a somber one.
BASH
(sigh)
Not yet.

it? I mean, come-on-- John’s your
best friend and didn’t say
anything? It’s been almost a
month. And now Tye? What did the

guard. Choosing his words wisely.
BASH

professional stress and couldn’t
take the pressure. Ran off
somewhere.
TODD
Stress? Are they kidding? You guys
are all a bunch of adrenaline
junkies! I bet they each found a
girlfriend then ran off.
BASH

complicate things. Besides it’s
against the rules.

Heading out. Away from the Trading Floor. Todd right
behind like a puppy.
TODD

have a drink with some of your
less fortunate comrades. Tell us
what we’re doing wrong. Relax a
little.

exact same kind Tye was wearing. But no blinking light.
BASH
Maybe next time. I gotta take care
of something.
TODD
Not that children’s charity thing
again? How much have you dumped
into that place? You’re crazy. Let
me know when you become a team
player.
Reluctantly exiting through a long hallway.
BASH
(under his breath)
That’ll never happen.
Staring back at the silent empty floor. Almost wishing it
were still going.

Making his way toward the kitchen. Passing a dozen
PICTURES of him and John, who’s the same age and build,

Forcing him to turn his head. Too painful to see.

BACKYARD.

in.
Bash tosses the MAIL onto an ever growing PILE next to a

Opening the sliding glass door.
Immediately greeted by the playful ANIMAL that runs

Removing a choice slab of BEEF.
BASH

really spoiled your ass.

Worth sixty-five million. The
house of course, get’s it’s

(proudly)

luck.
