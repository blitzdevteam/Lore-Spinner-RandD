ACT ONE
EXT. SYRIAN AIRSPACE - 0320 HOURS
Beautiful star filled sky under a hazy layer of darkness.
Inside this vast black void flies an unmarked, camouflage
Boeing C-17 transport AIRCRAFT cruising at 40,000 feet.
Its BACK RAMP flush with the fuselage. A RUMBLE, then--
slowly OPENS--
AIRCRAFT
Lined up at the edge of the RAMP-- a five man TEAM
dressed in HALO-HIGH ALTITUDE JUMP GEAR and BLACK
WET-SUITS. Also unmarked with no identifying insignias.
These guys could be from anywhere.
Across from them, safely tethered to the inner wall; two
FLIGHT ENGINEERS, both wearing OXYGEN MASKS, who toss
fully loaded camouflage BACKPACKS equipped with
PARACHUTES, out the opening.
One-by-one, each TEAM MEMBER follows the PACKS into the
night, spacing themselves by three seconds.
EXT. AIRCRAFT - NIGHT
The TEAM free fall for miles through endless darkness,
gaining speed-- reaching terminal velocity-- each MAN
above the other.
Tiny dots of twinkling light from the SYRIAN COASTLINE
slowly come into view as they rapidly descend toward the
now visible OCEAN quickly approaching--
Speeding past the OVERLOADED BACKPACKS gently floating to
earth under their automatically deployed CHUTES.
Cueing each MAN to check their glowing wrist altimeter,
deploying their PARACHUTES at their own specific
altitude.
All FIVE CHUTES open in sequence, spaced evenly apart,
dangerously close to the water--
SPLASH! Everyone enters hard, one-by-one.
Followed by the BACKPACKS that softly float down around
them.
Each MAN easily unhooks from their HARNESS and retrieves
a BACKPACK.

One of the MEN, “PAPA BEAR”, makes a quick head count,
giving a THUMBS UP to quietly swim toward shore, single
file.
EXT. JUBAYL BEACH - 0330 HOURS
Long sandy deserted stretch of coastline 40 miles south
of Tripoli. A thin TREELINE separates the beach from the
water twenty yards up.
All’s quiet--
Each MAN carefully emerges from the OCEAN, one behind the
other, looking bad-ass and scary, almost Alien.
The first two onto dry land hold MP-5 MACHINE GUNS, one
pointing left, the other right.
Both MEN in the middle carry the BACKPACKS, while the
last covers their rear.
Everyone stops in unison, kneeling in the sand, shedding
their OXYGEN MASKS and JUMP GEAR.
Giving us our first look at their young American faces,
colored in GREEN and BLACK grease paint--
E2 Airman ROBERT K. Stone (24) aka “Stoner” the Point Man
(PT), followed by E3 Airman First Class TROY C. BENNET
(26) aka “Playboy” the Compass Man (NAV), then E6 Tech
Sgt. JASON A. STILLS (32) aka “Papa Bear” Team Leader
(TL), then E2 Airman JACKSON K. PATRAKOS (25) aka
“Apollo” the Radio Operator (RO) and E2 Airman ROLAND R.
JIMENEZ (24) aka “Daredevil” the teams Rear Security
(RS).
They each hand Apollo their HALO GEAR, stashing it in a
hole he dug at the edge of the TREELINE, along with two
FULL PACKS, covering them with SAND.
Stoner carefully watches their flank like a predatory
bird. His finger ready on the trigger.
Papa Bear and Daredevil open the remaining BACKPACKS,
removing smaller ones, handing them out.
Playboy checks his GPS, verifying their position and
TARGET, giving Papa Bear an OK, pointing to the right of
the TREES.
The TEAM attach NIGHT VISION GOGGLES, secure their GEAR--
And wait for Papa Bear to wave his finger in a circle,
signaling everyone to move forward, single file.

EXT. COASTAL VILLAGE - NIGHT
Small and peaceful, considering most of the block wall
STRUCTURES are marked with BULLET HOLES and BOMB CRATERS.
This once beautiful and thriving seashore enclave
reduced to a ghost town.
Most of the village charm and soul now replaced by old
soviet TANKS and surplus ARTILLERY PIECES.
Insurgent FIGHTERS the only living inhabitants.
Two MALE BODIES hang from telephone poles. Their partial
decomposition tells us they’ve been there a while.
Every LOCAL either dead, enslaved or a forced
collaborator.
DUST CLOUDS between two bombed BUILDINGS rise up--
ARMED ISIS FIGHTERS in a Toyota PICK-UP speed out of
town, using SPOTLIGHTS to look for something-- or
someone.
TREELINE
Stoner leads the TEAM along a shallow and fowl smelling
DRAINAGE DITCH on the far side of town, closely following
Playboy’s directions.
Everyone using the chewed up TERRAIN as cover-- finding a
dozen SKELETAL REMAINS, still partially clothed, stacked
five feet high.
They have to ignore it despite what they feel, moving on,
until--
Sounds of BARKING, heading their way, getting CLOSER--
LOUDER.
Then they see it: A vicious PIT-BULL MIX running across
the street, heading right for them.
Passing THREE ISIS GUARDS who instantly take notice,
grabbing their WEAPONS and follow.
Stoner pulls a tiny WHISTLE from his glove and BLOWS--
Nothing. No sound of any kind.
But the DOG stops in its tracks, wincing, shaking its
head. It HOWLS and WHIMPERS, running back into town.

Not the FIGHTERS, who continue shinning their FLASHLIGHTS
into the darkness, dangerously close to the TEAM’S
position.
