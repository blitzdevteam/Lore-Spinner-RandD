ACT ONE
EXT. SAHARA DESERT - 2084
No man’s land. Only isolation and death. As an ominous
ORANGE SUN melts into a vast sea of nothing.
Off in the distance-- hundreds of long black VERTICAL
LINES SLOWLY APPEAR, streteching miles across the barren
wasteland. Perfectly straight and symmetrical. Looking
strangely odd for this remote part of the Globe.
High above the LINES at 28,000 feet, two small SQUADRONS
of WASTE DISPOSAL AIRCRAFT make their rapid decent.
Each massive FOUR ENGINE CARGO PLANE painted solid WHITE
with tails marked GWS.
INT. AIRCRAFT - DUSK

the entire aircraft.
CARGO BAY

Corporate executive KENNETH TRONSON (48), severely
beaten, but alive. Wearing a ripped and soiled GREY THREE
PIECE SUIT.
Frantically making his way down a MOUNTAIN OF GARBAGE.

It’s like trying to run in wet sand. And this guy’s no
athlete, sucking wind the entire way.
Reaching an equally beat-up but unconscious, BRADLEY

filth.

violently SHAKING HIM.

BACKWARD, starting from the TAIL SECTION gradually MOVING
FORWARD--
Creating a WIDE OPENING to the OUTSIDE. DUMPING the
GARBAGE.
BRADLEY
Go! Go!
Both men move as fast as they can, avoiding two story
AVALANCHES. Bradley fighting for his life through RIVERS
OF WASTE that flow around him.
Sweeping him away in a powerful debris laden CURRENT
OUTSIDE THE PLANE-- SCREAMING.

Panicked Kenneth won’t give up so easily. Climbing over
surging WASTE, toward a RUSTED STEEL LADDER bolted to the
fuselage ten yards away.
His only chance. Adrenaline pumping. Giving him the
second wind he desperately needs.
Almost there, reaching for a METAL RUNG. Grabbing it
tight. Holding on for dear life. Then yanked away by a
thick FLOW OF GARBAGE. Dragging him toward the OPENING--
And OUT THE AIRCRAFT. Falling to earth.
